The Diamondback terrapin eats hard-shelled prey, including aquatic snails, crabs and small bivalves, such as blue mussels and clams. Terrapins also eat carrion (dead flesh), fish, marine worms and insects. Diamondback Terrapins live on the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of the United States, from as far north as Cape Cod, Massachusetts to the southern tip of Florida and around the Gulf Coast to Texas.They are the only turtles believed to be in brackish water (Some salt, not as much as ocean), tidal marshes, estuaries and lagoon. Animals that live in the area of the Diamondback terrapin are raccoons,crabs, crows, gulls,herons, rats, muskrats, foxes, raccoons, skunks and mink. Lastly our Conservationist will talk about the Diamondback Terrapin. Most terrapins hibernate during the winter by burrowing into the mud of marshes.
